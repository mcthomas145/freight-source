Inside the board

More than a list of loads.

Here's what's working under the hood every time you search.

01

Multi-source aggregation

Freight Source connects to DAT, Truckstop.com, 123Loadboard, Sylectus and your own broker network, then merges every posting into a single board and removes duplicates — so each load appears once, with its source clearly labeled.

02

Qualified, Near Miss & Hidden

Results sort themselves automatically: Qualified loads meet your search and your standards, Near Miss loads sit just outside one filter so you don't miss a good option, and Hidden holds the loads and brokers you've chosen to suppress.

03

Advanced search & saved searches

Filter by origin, destination, deadhead radius, equipment, weight, length, rate, revenue-per-mile, profit and posting age. Save any combination as a named tab and keep a live, refreshing feed of matching freight.

04

Graded and priced at a glance

Every row shows the load's A–D grade, its rate and revenue-per-mile, the posting source, the broker, the age and the total miles — so you can scan and compare without opening a thing.

05

Offer or book in one click

Send an offer, fire back a counter, or book a load straight from the board, and follow the whole negotiation thread in one place.

Quality grades

Every load, graded to your standards.

A rate means nothing until it's measured against your costs. Freight Source grades each load from A to D using your break-even, target margin, and lane preferences — so good freight rises to the top.

A
Strong margin
Well above your target rate per mile with healthy profit to spare.
B
Solid
Clears your break-even comfortably and meets your lane standards.
C
Thin
Covers costs but leaves little upside — book it only if it fits the plan.
D
Below standard
Falls under your minimums. Flagged so you never book it by accident.

Set your standards once. Enter your break-even cost per mile, target RPM, and the lanes you favor in the Cost Calculator — every load on the board is then measured against them automatically, and re-grades the moment fuel or market rates move.

How grading works

A grade you can actually trust.

Grades are calculated for your operation — not a generic market average.

01

Built from your real costs

Freight Source scores each load using your break-even cost per mile, your target revenue-per-mile, and the lanes you prefer to run. The grade reflects what a load is worth to you, specifically.

02

A clear A-to-D scale

A means strong margin, B clears your costs comfortably, C is thin, and D falls below your minimums. One glance tells you whether a load is worth a closer look.

03

Re-grades as the market moves

Fuel prices and lane rates change all day, and grades change with them. A load that was a B this morning can become an A when diesel drops or the lane tightens up.

04

Set your standards once

Enter your numbers in the Cost Calculator a single time. From then on every load on every search is measured against them automatically — no spreadsheets, no manual math.

05

Never book a bad load by accident

D-grade loads are flagged so they can't slip through unnoticed. The profitable freight rises to the top and the rest gets out of your way.

Built for the dispatch desk

Everything you need to book the right load.

Advanced load search

Filter freight by lane, deadhead radius, equipment, rate, revenue-per-mile, profit, weight, length and posting age. Combine filters, save them as named searches, and watch a live feed of new matches roll in throughout the day.

Every broker's credit score, average days-to-pay and your full history sit in one place. Favorite the brokers who pay fast, flag the ones to avoid, and check a broker's track record before you commit to a load.
